Ring Theory Basics:

When someone in your cirlces is ill or aggrieved, send comfort toward them and anyone closer to them than you are. If you need to unload stress, dump out toward farther friends.

Seems obvious,
but also easy to forget.

Rings Applied to Politics:

Listening, comfort and solidarity should go inward.

Expect both leadership and a need to dump stress to come from inner circles outward.

Part 1, Media: Give voice to the center circle

Ex: If someone else fears a family member may be deported and you don't, simply share their voice. Echo them by keeping your voice, your ego, and your need to process to a minimum.

orig article

Part 1, Media: Don't Muffle the Consequences

Do you want your listeners to argue with you sitting at your keyboard, or face the direct and personal consequences of their political actions? Don't muffle first-person voices by adding your keyboard-judgment.

Part 1, Media: Keep a List of Voices to Echo

It's more effective, more fair, and even easier.

Keep a list of people speaking in the first person, simply share those articles when you need to comment.

Part 1B, Media: Echo the center circle

Find religious leaders, conservatives, military experts or others who agree on this policy. Don't score points; no one is keeping score. Change happens when we are on the same side.

Part 2, Social:
Support the center

Dumping your stress and anger can't replace a medical plan or political social media plan. Dealing with stress and anger is absolutely fair and normal part of life — offer comfort and solidarity.

geek out: On Both Hands: Anger

Part 2, Social:
Hugs, Not Echo Chambers

Sometimes we need hugs, and all we have is facebook. Don't police your friend's anger. But don't spread it beyond your friend circle — show up or private message them, rather than try to push facebook's algorithm to give angry posts maximum exposure.

geek out: On Both Hands: Anger

Part 2, Social:
Listening is Work

When someone famous dumps their anger, they are asking for a lot of people to do the work of supporting them. This is backward. Make sure everyone is heard by the circle that can support them. Don't be afraid to ask for support, to ask people to listen to you; simply dump out, comfort in.
